# 🧠 Theme 1: Data Foundations, Quality Frameworks & Bias Control

## 🏢 Executive Overview
Before building any complex dashboard or machine learning model, a business must establish a rock-solid foundation of data integrity. This module demonstrates how to take chaotic operational observations, clean out system noise, and eliminate human cognitive shortcuts during critical team meetings.

## 🔍 Deep-Dive Project Breakdowns

### 1. Operations Audit: The University Coffee Shop Bottleneck
*   **The Academic Source:** `Ejercicio1_Tema1.docx`[cite: 4]
*   **The Business Problem:** A high-traffic campus coffee shop faces long morning queues and dropping reviews, yet food revenue is flatlining. 
*   **The Core Analytics Framework (WHAT / HOW / WHY):**
    *   **WHAT (The Behavior):** Data shows a massive transaction surge between 08:00 and 09:00 AM[cite: 4]. Customers look intently at the sandwich display case but exit with only black coffee[cite: 4].
    *   **HOW (The Friction):** Timestamps reveal that the operational line delay stretches up to 11 minutes. The user experience drops severely as customers experience morning rush anxiety[cite: 4].
    *   **WHY (The Bottleneck):** Students running late cannot wait 6 minutes for a kitchen worker to toast a custom sandwich. They abandon their food craving to guarantee making it to class on time[cite: 4].
*   **The Strategic Decision:** Transition operations into an agile, customer-centric framework. Pre-package high-volume cold items into a dedicated "Grab & Go" morning kiosk and deploy mobile POS tablets to bust lines before customers hit the register.

### 2. Profit Optimization: E-Commerce Inventory Margin Efficiency
*   **The Academic Source:** `Ejercicio2_Tema1.docx`[cite: 3]
*   **The Business Problem:** A digital storefront has a €500 weekend ad budget. The owner wants to push "Headphones" because they have the highest price tag and bring in the largest volume of gross top-line cash flow[cite: 3].
*   **The Statistical Reality Check:**
    *   **Headphones:** 300 visits, 15 sales (€750 revenue) $\rightarrow$ **5.0% Conversion Rate**[cite: 3].
    *   **Reusable Bottles:** 150 visits, 30 sales (€450 revenue) $\rightarrow$ **20.0% Conversion Rate**[cite: 3].
*   **The Strategic Decision:** Do not chase the highest price tag blindly. The Headphones require **20 website visits** to close one deal, whereas Reusable Bottles convert at **4x that speed** (only 5 visits per sale)[cite: 3]. We route the marketing budget to the Reusable Bottle to generate rapid inventory turnover and secure short-term operational cash flow.

### 3. Data Refinement: Transforming Social Media Chaos into Structure
*   **The Academic Source:** `Ejercicio1_DIKW_Tema2.xlsx`
*   **The Business Problem:** Customer feedback on social platforms is unstructured, highly qualitative, and unorganized. 
*   **The Solution Engine:** Applying the **DIKW Pyramid** (Data $\rightarrow$ Information $\rightarrow$ Knowledge $\rightarrow$ Wisdom) to map metadata strings, filter invalid tracking tokens, and aggregate customer complaints into quantifiable sentiment bins.

## 🛡️ Strategic De-Biasing Playbook
When teams sit down in growth meetings, hidden cognitive biases frequently destroy capital. This portfolio uses data to actively dismantle four classic organizational traps:

1.  **Anchoring Bias (Sesgo de Anclaje):** The team tries to price a new shoe at €89 just because a competitor sells theirs at €90[cite: 1]. *The Cure:* Reject external anchors. Build pricing models on internal cost of goods sold (COGS), margin parameters, and historical price elasticity.
2.  **Availability Bias (Sesgo de Disponibilidad):** A manager shouts, *"This product will be a viral hit, I saw three TikTok videos about it today!"*[cite: 1] *The Cure:* Viral occurrences are statistical anomalies. We enforce a review of systematic keyword search volumes and trend data.
3.  **Social Conformity (Sesgo de Conformity Social):** Team members stay quiet about structural product flaws to preserve meeting harmony[cite: 1]. *The Cure:* Establish anonymous submission forms or assign a rotating "Devil's Advocate" to stress-test ideas before sign-off.
4.  **Confirmation Bias (Sesgo de Confirmación):** Filtering for beautiful 5-star comments while ignoring complaints about splitting product seams[cite: 1]. *The Cure:* Mandate a weekly review matching units sold against raw return rates and 1-star product reviews.
